Step 1 — The Holder / Wallet

Your Wallet stores all your credentials. When someone asks for proof, you pick which credentials to share, sign the response with your personal key, and send it — nobody can impersonate you.

📋 What happens when someone asks for your data?

Think of it like showing your ID at a bar. You don't hand the bouncer your passport for keeps — you flash it, they check it, and you put it away. A digital wallet works the same way.

💼
Your credentials live here — every credential you've collected (university degree, government ID, medical records) is stored locally in your wallet.
📨
Someone asks for proof — a bar, employer, or app sends a request like "prove you're over 21". You see exactly what they want before anything is shared.
🎯
You pick which credentials to share — only the ones relevant to the request go in. Everything else stays in the wallet.
✍️
You sign and send — the wallet bundles your chosen credentials into a presentation and signs it with your personal key. That signature proves the verifier is talking to you, so a stolen credential alone is useless.
🔗 Why this matters for Truecaller
  • Branding & Trust — By acting as a credential wallet, Truecaller positions itself as a company that takes user security and privacy seriously.
  • Monetization — Truecaller can sell ads or choose to provide a quality service to win the trust of users much like googles authentication phone application.
